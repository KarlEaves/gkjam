Scales

Learning the basics of scales is one of the most important concepts in learning to jam along with your friends, favorite artists, and backing tracks. If you know what scale a song is in, you can easily jam along!

Before diving into learning scales, you should have a basic understanding musical notes. Traditional music consists of 12 notes, or semitones - A, A#, B, C, C#, D, D#, E, F, F#, G, and G#. Each note is a half step, or semitone, away from the next note. A whole step is two semitones away. Let's look at an example. If we start at A, we see that A# is a half step or semitone away. B is a whole step, or two semitones, away.

Scales are defined by their KEY and TYPE. The key of a scale is determined by the root note. If your root note is C, you are playing a C scale. The type of scale is defined by the distance between each note in the scale, and drastically changes the way the music sounds. Let's explore this concept a bit.

For example, major scales, which produce a happy sound, start with the root note and go up by a whole step, whole step, half step, whole step, whole step, whole step, and half step back to the root.

Minor scales, which produce a more somber sound, start with the root note, go up by a whole step, half step, whole step, whole step, half step, whole step, and finally a whole step back to the root.
